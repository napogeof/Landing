What is an Everesting?


Everesting is an endurance cycling challenge with a pretty simple concept: climb with your bike any hill, repeat the same climb until you reach the height of Mt. Everest at (8848m) 29,032 ft.

To complete an Everesting Challenge, the cyclist must accumulate an elevation gain of 8848m/29029ft, in one hill of any length, also, it must be recorded with GPS in one single activity. There is no time limit, but no sleeping is allowed.

For full details on the Everesting Challenge check out their official page Everesting.cc

Daniel, our cyclist, completed his everesting challenge on December 17, 2021. He accumulated 8893 m of elevation gain and 219 km of distance in a 30 hours 2 min activity. The route was an iconic climb in suburban Santiago de Chile called Farellones, a road that leads to the ski centers near the city at approximately 2410 m.a.s.l. That was one of the hottest day of the year and Daniel had to ride with 41 degrees Celsius during the day.

Check the route and more details of the ride in Daniel’s Strava or check the Hall of Fame at the Everesting’s website

The Story


Since I started dreaming about an everesting, I thought that this was a transformational experience. Knowing that it was going to be tough both physical and mentally, I used to say that I will start an everesting like one person and finish it like different one. I also visualized it as a tool to face my demons, all the physical challenge of it was like cycling through hell.

Then it hit me! What if I picture myself going through the circles of Hell? Just like in the Divine Comedy, Dante travels through each circle until he arrives at the purgatory?. It was just like that!, I faced my demons at night, endured burning 41 Celsius (106 F) during the day, resisted sleep deprivation, I even had a couple of hallucinations.

The NFTs


In collaboration with some friends, we are turning the experience into 9 NFTs, inspired by Dante’s version of Hell, each piece represents each climb combined my thoughts and feelings during the challenge.

This is not a project to make money, our mission is to inspire other people to challenge themselves through sports. With funds of the sales we will help organizations helping young people and local communities in Venezuela using cycling and other sports. We will drop 200 editions of each NFT piece and 60% of the funds will be donated to an organization chosen by our community in Twitter.

The NFTs will be dropped in DoinGud, an amazing platform putting social causes as the heart of the NFT movement, funds will be allocated as follows:

60% to cycling/sport/education projects in Venezuela (Our homeland)

5% to charity within DoinGud

14% to cyclist and project creator

7% to project producer

7% to the NFT artist

7% to the sherpa and photographer


What is next?


Each NFT will also give you access to the Proof of Sweat (PoSW). This is an archive of media produced during the challenge as proof that Daniel actually did the everesting challenge

Also owning an Everesting Hell NFT will give you early access to the Proof of Sweat Community. This will be the next step in our NFT journey, the evolution of this project to become a community of endurance/NFT challenges. More details on this soon!
